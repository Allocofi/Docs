## Market-Neutral Risk Controls

The strategy is designed to minimize directional exposure through hedged positions.

Portfolio exposure is monitored through:

- Net market exposure
- Gross exposure
- Delta exposure
- Asset concentration
- Exchange exposure

The objective is to generate returns primarily from market inefficiencies rather than market direction.

## Quantitative Risk Framework

The strategy uses systematic monitoring and predefined risk parameters.

Risk controls include:

- Automated position monitoring
- Exposure limits
- Trade execution controls
- Liquidity assessment
- Real-time portfolio monitoring

Positions may be adjusted or reduced when risk conditions exceed predefined thresholds.

## Execution & Counterparty Risk

Because the strategy interacts with multiple trading venues, risk management includes:

- Exchange diversification
- Counterparty monitoring
- Withdrawal and operational controls
- Trading limit management
- Execution quality monitoring

The strategy seeks to reduce dependency on any single venue or liquidity source.

## Liquidity Management

USDA liquidity is supported by actively traded digital asset markets and systematic position management.

Redemption processing depends on:

- Market liquidity conditions
- Position unwinding requirements
- Settlement timelines
- Trading venue availability

The redemption value is calculated based on the applicable NAV:

$$
Redemption\ Value
=
Redeemed\ Units
\times
NAV_{per\ Unit}
-
Applicable\ Fees
$$

During periods of extreme market volatility or abnormal liquidity conditions, processing times may be extended to ensure orderly execution.

## Key Risks

Investors should consider the following risks:

- Digital asset market volatility
- Exchange and counterparty risk
- Funding rate fluctuations
- Execution and slippage risk
- Liquidity risk
- Model and algorithm risk
- Operational and infrastructure risk
