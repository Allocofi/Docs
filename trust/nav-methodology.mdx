NAV (Net Asset Value) is the core valuation reference for every Alloco ETP product. It drives issuance pricing, redemption settlement, secondary market anchoring, RFQ pricing, risk control, and disclosure reporting. Alloco uses a three-layer NAV system designed to balance accuracy, timeliness, and risk protection — so that the right valuation logic applies at every stage of a product's lifecycle.

## Three-Layer NAV System

Alloco calculates three distinct types of NAV, each serving a specific purpose. Understanding the difference between them helps you interpret prices, reports, and system states accurately.

| NAV Type | Main Use | Update Frequency | Core Feature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Official NAV** | Formal settlement, primary create/redeem, daily report, audit and investor reporting | Usually daily or after the product settlement cycle | Highest accuracy; based on full holdings, cash, fees, liabilities and corporate-action checks |
| **iNAV (Indicative NAV)** | Secondary market price anchoring, RFQ quote reference and premium/discount monitoring | May update every 15–60 seconds during active markets | More real-time; depends on data feeds, order books, TWAP, position snapshots and cash estimates |
| **Risk NAV** | Risk control, suspension, quote protection, redemption limits and emergency mode | Real-time or event-driven | More conservative; includes stale price, liquidity, custody, counterparty and closed-market haircuts |

## Core Calculation Formula

Every NAV calculation starts from the same foundational formula. All three NAV types follow this structure, differing only in data freshness, haircut levels, and validation completeness.

**Total NAV:**

```
Total NAV = asset market value + cash + receivables - liabilities - accrued fees - cost reserves - risk discounts
```

**NAV per Token:**

```
NAV per Token = Total NAV / Total Token Supply
```

Asset value may include spot assets, equities, ETFs, derivatives, perpetual-contract margin, swap exposure, stablecoins, treasury bills, or cash — depending on the product's eligible asset universe. Liabilities may include pending redemptions, accrued fees, trading costs, financing costs, taxes, custody expenses, audit fees, or other product-level obligations.

## Data Inputs

Accurate NAV depends on high-quality, validated data across several categories. Each input type has defined validation rules, and failures at any layer can change the NAV state.

| Data Type | Examples | Validation |
|---|---|---|
| **Price data** | Primary feed, secondary feed, TWAP, broker quote | Multi-source deviation checks; if thresholds exceeded, enter Degraded state |
| **Position data** | Onchain balance, CEX position, broker statement | Compare with previous snapshot; abnormal changes require manual or multisig confirmation |
| **Cash and payables** | USDC, USD, pending trades, fees | Onchain balance checks and reconciliation with bank/custodian/administrator reports |
| **Corporate actions/events** | Dividends, splits, halts, mergers, liquidations | Match event calendars; restrict real-time create/redeem if processing incomplete |
| **Risk parameters** | Haircut, status, confidence | Generated by rules engine; material changes recorded with approval logs |

## Risk NAV and Haircuts

Risk NAV is a more conservative valuation layer applied when uncertainty increases. It incorporates haircuts — downward adjustments to asset value — to protect the product's asset pool and existing token holders during uncertain or stressed conditions.

| Haircut Type | Trigger Condition | Example Range |
|---|---|---|
| **Liquidity Haircut** | Order book thin, AMM depth declines or underlying liquidity deteriorates | 0–100 bps or higher |
| **Stale Price Haircut** | Price update exceeds defined freshness threshold | 10–100 bps |
| **Closed-Market Haircut** | Underlying market is closed, on holiday or not executable | Product-specific |
| **Counterparty Haircut** | Custodian, broker, CEX or swap counterparty risk rises | 10–100 bps |
| **Event Haircut** | Halt, major announcement, corporate action, onchain attack or stablecoin depeg | Case-by-case |
| **FX/Settlement Haircut** | Cross-currency, cross-market or cross-time-zone settlement uncertainty | 10–100 bps |

Risk NAV may be lower than iNAV or Official NAV. Its purpose is to protect users, the product asset pool, and system stability during uncertain states — not to reflect a precise fair value.

## NAV State Machine

The NAV engine operates as a state machine. The current state determines which actions are permitted, which pricing references apply, and what restrictions are in force. The system transitions between states automatically based on data quality, market conditions, and risk events.

| State | Meaning | Allowed Actions | Restrictions |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fresh** | Data current, price sources normal, holdings reconciled | Normal trading, RFQ, create/redeem | Regular limits and fees |
| **Stale** | NAV or key price update delayed | Small trades and limited RFQ | Restrict large create/redeem, widen spread |
| **Frozen** | Underlying market closed or halted | Small trades or view-only | Suspend large instant create/redeem |
| **Degraded** | Data-source conflict, lower-liquidity or abnormal price | Limited trading and limited redemption | Increase haircut, suspend high-risk paths |
| **Emergency** | Oracle failure, custody event, extreme deviation or major security event | Usually view-only or redeem-only | Suspend secondary trading, RFQ and creation |

<Warning>
Alloco does not guarantee that the secondary market price of a product token will always equal NAV. NAV may be delayed, estimated, adjusted or wrong due to data source issues, oracle failures, or valuation exceptions.
</Warning>

## NAV Reports

Every material NAV update generates a traceable, versioned report. You can use these reports to verify the valuation behind a product at any given point in time.

Each NAV report contains:

- Product name, ticker, and valuation time
- Calculation version and methodology reference
- Total NAV and NAV per Token
- Total token supply
- Cash, liabilities, and accrued fees
- Applied haircuts and adjustments
- Holdings summary or exposure breakdown
- Data-source quality indicators
- Current NAV state and confidence level
- Create/redeem availability at time of calculation
- Report hash, signer, and approval records

<Note>
NAV reports are the authoritative record for formal settlement and auditing. If there is any discrepancy between NAV shown on the platform interface and the corresponding NAV report, the report controls.
</Note>
