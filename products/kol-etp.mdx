Executing a comprehensive investment thesis independently requires navigating high operational friction—opening multiple exchange accounts, manually executing orders, managing private keys, and constantly rebalancing positions. **Alloco ETFTs streamline this entire lifecycle into a unified, single-token position.** Instead of manually constructing and maintaining a multi-asset portfolio, investors gain immediate, transparent exposure to curated strategies, indices, or themes through a single transaction.

|  | Alloco ETP | Direct Underlying Exposure |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Access** | Access a complete portfolio, strategy, theme, or index through one product | Open multiple accounts and purchase each asset separately |
| **Portfolio Construction** | Exposure is packaged according to predefined rules or a manager’s methodology | Investors must construct and maintain the portfolio themselves |
| **Rebalancing** | Portfolio adjustments are handled at the product level | Investors must monitor and rebalance each position manually |
| **Capital Efficiency** | Gain diversified exposure through a single subscription | Capital must be allocated across multiple assets and venues |
| **Transparency** | NAV, holdings, methodology, fees, and key risks are presented in one place | Information is fragmented across brokers, exchanges, and individual assets |
| **Operations** | Custody, execution, settlement, and corporate actions are managed within the product structure | Investors manage wallets, brokers, settlement, and asset-specific events themselves |
| **Liquidity & Exit** | Trade through supported markets or redeem under the applicable product terms | Each underlying position must be sold separately, potentially across different markets |
| **User Experience** | One product, one position, one performance view | Multiple assets, accounts, transactions, and performance records |

<Danger>
  An ETP does not eliminate the market risks of its underlying assets. It provides a different structure for accessing and managing that exposure.
</Danger>
