## Position-Level Controls

Individual positions may be subject to:

- Position-size limits
- Stop-loss thresholds
- Exposure limits
- Instrument-level risk limits
- Predefined entry and exit rules

These controls are intended to prevent a single trade or market event from having a disproportionate effect on the portfolio.

## Portfolio-Level Controls

At the portfolio level, risk may be monitored through:

- Gross and net exposure
- Currency concentration
- Precious-metals exposure
- Portfolio volatility
- Drawdown levels
- Liquidity requirements
- Counterparty exposure

The portfolio may reduce exposure, hedge positions or hold additional cash when market conditions become less favourable.

## Liquidity

The underlying markets are generally liquid, but redemption timing may depend on the ability to close or settle active positions in an orderly manner.

Redemption requests may therefore be subject to:

- Scheduled redemption windows
- Processing periods
- Market liquidity
- Settlement timing
- Temporary restrictions during exceptional market conditions

The estimated redemption value is calculated using the applicable NAV after trading costs, fees and settlement adjustments.

$$
Redemption\ Value
=
Redeemed\ Units
\times
Final\ NAV\ per\ Unit
-
Applicable\ Costs
$$
