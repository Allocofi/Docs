Alloco ETP products involve several types of fees and costs. Understanding the full fee structure helps you evaluate the true cost of accessing and holding a product. Some fees are deducted from NAV automatically, while others are charged at the point of transaction or paid directly to the network. Read through each category below so you know what to expect before you subscribe, trade, or redeem.

## Fee Types

The table below describes each category of fee or cost you may encounter across Alloco products.

| Fee Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Product management fee | Charged based on product net assets to cover management, infrastructure, issuance, reporting, and operations |
| Creation/redemption fee | Charged when product shares are created or redeemed in the primary market |
| Execution cost | Trading fees, slippage, broker fees, gas, exchange fees, and hedging/rebalancing costs |
| Market-making and routing cost | Costs or spreads related to RFQ, MM, AP, solver, order routing, or liquidity aggregation |
| Custody and administration cost | Custody, fund administration, audit, legal, data source, oracle, NAV calculation, and reporting costs |

## How Fees Are Charged

Fees are collected at different points in the product lifecycle depending on their type. They may be deducted from:

- **The applicable portfolio or NAV** — management fees are typically accrued and deducted at the product level, which means they reduce NAV over time rather than appearing as a separate line item on your statement.
- **Subscription proceeds** — subscription or minting fees are deducted from the assets you transfer when you subscribe.
- **Redemption proceeds** — redemption fees are deducted from the value paid out when you redeem units.
- **Your connected wallet** — network gas fees are paid directly by you when you sign blockchain transactions.

## Gas and Network Fees

Every blockchain transaction — including subscriptions, redemptions, claims, and secondary market swaps — requires a gas fee paid in the native token of the relevant network (for example, ETH on Ethereum). Gas fees are entirely separate from Alloco product fees and fluctuate with network congestion. You are responsible for maintaining a sufficient balance of the relevant network token in your wallet to cover gas costs.

<Note>
  You are responsible for third-party fees and network charges even where a transaction fails, unless expressly stated otherwise in the product documents.
</Note>

## Where to Find Product-Specific Fees

Fee schedules vary by product. You can find the exact fees that apply to any Alloco product in the following places:

- The product page on [app.allocofi.com](https://app.allocofi.com)
- The product prospectus or offering documents
- The NAV methodology document, which explains how fees are reflected in the NAV calculation

<Tip>
  Review the full fee schedule in the Product Documents before subscribing. Fees are deducted from NAV, which means they reduce the value of your holding over time.
</Tip>

## Execution Costs in Secondary Trading

When you buy or sell ETP Units on secondary markets, you also incur execution costs that are separate from the product fees described above. These include:

- **DEX trading fees and AMM slippage** — charged by the decentralised exchange protocol and affected by pool depth and order size
- **RFQ spread (bid-ask)** — the difference between the price at which a market maker buys and sells, embedded in the quote you receive
- **Network gas fees** — charged by the blockchain for each transaction you sign

These are not Alloco fees, but they are real costs that affect your total cost of entry and exit. Factor them into your analysis alongside the product management fee and any subscription or redemption charges.
