Understanding how your product assets are held is a key part of evaluating any investment on Alloco. This page explains the custody structure used for different product types, how assets are segregated from the operating company and from other products, and what operational controls are in place to protect those assets.

## Core Principle

Product assets should be recorded separately from operating-company assets, other product assets, and service-provider proprietary assets wherever possible. This separation exists to protect your economic interest in a product — even in scenarios involving platform failure, service-provider insolvency, or operational exceptions.

<Note>
You should rely on the legal structure, custody records, NAV reports, and reconciliation processes described in the product documents — not only on platform statements or UI displays — when assessing how your investment is held.
</Note>

## Custody by Asset Type

Different asset types require different custody arrangements. The table below summarises the possible holding methods and primary record sources for each category.

| Asset/Exposure Type | Possible Holding Method | Main Records |
|---|---|---|
| **Onchain assets** | Smart contract vault, multisig wallet or compliant custody wallet | Onchain balance, transaction hash and vault records |
| **Stablecoins/cash** | Vault, custody account, bank account or issuer account | Onchain balance, bank/custodian report and cash ledger |
| **Equities/ETFs/traditional assets** | Brokerage account, custodian, SPV/SPC asset pool | Broker statement, custody statement and asset-pool ledger |
| **Derivatives/perpetuals/swaps** | Exchange account, prime broker or swap counterparty | Position report, margin record and PnL report |
| **RWA/other structured assets** | SPV, fund, trust or other legal vehicle | Legal register, asset list and manager report |

## SPV and Asset Pool Structure

Alloco-related products may be supported by SPVs (Special Purpose Vehicles), SPCs (Special Purpose Companies), segregated portfolios, fund entities, or other applicable legal vehicles. The purpose of these structures is to separate a specific product's underlying assets, cash, receivables, payables, and rights/obligations from the Alloco operating company and from other products.

The roles within this structure are defined as follows:

- The **operating company** provides technology, front end, protocol, data, product operation, or service support
- The **SPV/SPC or asset pool** holds or arranges to hold underlying assets, cash, and related rights
- **Custodians, brokers, administrators, managers, or other service providers** provide custody, execution, valuation, and reporting support
- The **product token** acts as an operational record and transfer rail; its legal nature depends on the product documents and your eligibility classification

## Onchain Asset Controls

For products that hold onchain assets, the following technical controls govern how those assets are managed:

- Vault contracts limit mint/burn, asset transfer, fee collection, and emergency pause permissions
- Multisig or MPC wallets use signing thresholds, operation logs, address whitelists, and per-transaction amount limits
- Major contract upgrades use timelock, multisig approval, or guardian mechanisms before taking effect
- Onchain balances are reconciled with NAV reports, product token supply, and user share records on a regular basis

## Holding Methods

Products may use one or more of the following holding structures depending on underlying asset type and product design.

| Holding Method | Description |
|---|---|
| **Direct holding** | The asset pool directly holds stablecoins, tokens, equities, ETFs or cash |
| **Indirect holding** | The asset pool obtains economic exposure through a fund, SPV, note, swap, synthetic tool or custody arrangement |
| **Margin holding** | Strategy products maintain margin and hedge positions at an exchange, broker or prime broker |
| **Cash buffer** | A product retains cash or short-term liquidity assets for fees, redemptions, slippage and exception states |
| **Risk reserve** | Certain products maintain cost reserves for trading costs, closed-market risk, counterparty risk or redemption processing |

## Third-Party Service Providers

Many custody and execution functions are performed by third-party service providers. Each provider type carries its own risk profile, which is reflected in the product's risk disclosures and Risk NAV haircut model.

| Service Provider | Possible Responsibilities | Main Risks |
|---|---|---|
| **Custodian** | Hold securities, cash, stablecoins or other assets | Bankruptcy, freeze, operational error or legal dispute |
| **Broker/PB** | Execute equities, ETFs, derivatives or financing trades | Order rejection, delay, margin change or settlement failure |
| **Exchange/CEX** | Execute crypto trades, perpetual contracts or funding-rate strategies | Withdrawal limits, bankruptcy, hacking or liquidation risk |
| **Market maker/AP** | Quote, hold inventory, create/redeem and repair premium/discount | Insufficient inventory, quote withdrawal or performance failure |
| **Oracle/data source** | Provide prices, market state and NAV inputs | Delay, error, manipulation or outage |
| **Administrator/manager** | Support reporting, registration, reconciliation and wind-down | Operational error, delay or file inconsistency |

<Warning>
Alloco does not guarantee the solvency, security, availability, or performance of any third-party custodian, broker, bank, exchange, protocol, or counterparty.
</Warning>

## What You Can Review

Custody-related disclosure for each product is available on the relevant product page on [app.allocofi.com](https://app.allocofi.com). For each product, you can review:

- The product asset-pool structure and legal wrapper type
- Main custody or brokerage arrangements
- Asset-segregation principles applicable to the product
- Vault or contract addresses (where applicable for onchain holdings)
- Recent NAV reports and report hashes
- Cash buffer levels and pending redemption amounts
- Any exception, suspension, or wind-down notices in effect
