Alloco uses digital wallets for authentication, transaction signing, and ETP Unit management. You may connect a self-custodial wallet you already own, or use an embedded wallet created through the Alloco platform. Understanding wallet security is critical before interacting with any product — your wallet is your gateway to every transaction on the platform, and many actions on a blockchain are irreversible once confirmed.

## Connecting Your Wallet

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit app.allocofi.com">
    Open [app.allocofi.com](https://app.allocofi.com) in your browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click 'Connect Wallet'">
    Select **Connect Wallet** in the top navigation bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your wallet type">
    Select either a self-custodial wallet (e.g., MetaMask or Coinbase Wallet) or an embedded wallet created directly through Alloco.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve the connection request">
    Your wallet will prompt you to approve the connection. Review the request carefully, then confirm it within your wallet interface.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete account setup or KYC verification">
    Depending on the products you want to access, you may be guided through additional account setup steps or identity verification. Follow any on-screen instructions to complete onboarding.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supported Wallet Types

Alloco supports two categories of digital wallet:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Self-Custodial Wallets" icon="wallet">
    Wallets you own and control independently, such as MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, or any other compatible wallet. You hold your own private keys and are entirely responsible for their security.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Embedded Wallets" icon="key">
    Wallets created directly through the Alloco platform using your email address, a passkey, or a third-party authentication provider. These are designed for a simpler onboarding experience while still giving you control over your assets.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Both wallet types allow you to interact with Alloco's ETP products. The right choice depends on your technical experience and security preferences.

## Your Wallet Responsibilities

As a wallet holder, you are solely responsible for the following. Alloco cannot intervene on your behalf if any of these areas are neglected.

- **Selecting and maintaining your wallet** — choose a reputable wallet provider and keep your software up to date.
- **Protecting private keys, seed phrases, passwords, and devices** — store these securely and never share them with anyone.
- **Confirming wallet addresses before sending transactions** — always double-check the destination address; blockchain transactions cannot be reversed.
- **Reviewing transaction details carefully before signing** — verify amounts, fees, and contract addresses before you confirm.
- **Understanding wallet permissions and token approvals** — be aware of what each approval grants before accepting it.
- **Maintaining sufficient network tokens for gas fees** — you need a small balance of the native network token to cover transaction costs.
- **Ensuring your wallet is compatible with the applicable blockchain and smart contracts** — not all wallets support every network or contract standard.

<Warning>
  Never provide your private key or seed phrase to Alloco or any person claiming to represent Alloco. Alloco cannot recover private keys, reverse wallet transactions, restore access to a lost wallet, cancel a confirmed blockchain transaction, or retrieve assets sent to an incorrect address or unsupported network.
</Warning>

## Wallet Signatures

When you interact with Alloco products, you will be asked to sign transactions or messages using your wallet. A wallet signature may constitute a legally binding instruction — similar in effect to signing a document — and may be irreversible once submitted to a blockchain.

Before confirming any signature, review the details carefully:

- Check that the contract address matches the expected Alloco or product smart contract.
- Verify that the amounts and parameters are correct.
- Confirm that you understand what permission or action the signature authorises.

If anything looks unfamiliar or unexpected, do not sign. Contact [support@allocofi.com](mailto:support@allocofi.com) if you are unsure.

## Network Tokens and Gas

Every blockchain transaction requires a gas fee paid in the native token of the network you are using. Gas fees are paid to the network itself — not to Alloco — and are required regardless of whether your transaction succeeds or fails.

Before interacting with Alloco products on any chain, make sure your wallet holds a small amount of the relevant network token:

| Network | Native Token |
|---|---|
| Ethereum | ETH |
| Polygon | POL (formerly MATIC) |
| Base | ETH |
| Arbitrum | ETH |
| Other EVM chains | Refer to that chain's documentation |

Gas costs vary with network congestion. Keep a buffer in your wallet to avoid failed transactions during periods of high demand.

## Security Best Practices

<Tip>
  Follow these practices to keep your wallet and assets as secure as possible:

  - **Use a hardware wallet** (e.g., Ledger or Trezor) for large or long-term holdings — hardware wallets keep your private key offline and away from potential attackers.
  - **Never share your seed phrase with anyone** — not with support agents, not in online forms, not with anyone who contacts you claiming to be from Alloco.
  - **Verify contract addresses before approving transactions** — check addresses against official Alloco documentation before interacting with any smart contract.
  - **Use a dedicated wallet for DeFi interactions** — keeping a separate wallet for active on-chain activity limits your exposure if that wallet is ever compromised.
  - **Regularly review and revoke unnecessary token approvals** — use a tool like Revoke.cash to audit and remove approvals you no longer need.
</Tip>

<Note>
  Alloco does not control self-custodial wallets and generally cannot assist with wallet recovery, private key management, or direct blockchain interactions outside the platform interface.
</Note>
