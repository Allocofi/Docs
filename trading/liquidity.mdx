Alloco products are designed with multiple liquidity layers to serve different order types and market conditions. Understanding how liquidity works helps you choose the right access route and manage execution risk. Liquidity for any given product depends on its underlying asset type, NAV state, and the market makers or authorized participants supporting it at any given time.

## Liquidity Architecture

Alloco products draw on two main backing types, each with its own liquidity profile and access routes.

### Crypto-Native Backing

Products backed by crypto-native assets use spot positions, perpetual contracts, and onchain positions as their underlying holdings. These products operate on weekly settlement and rebalance cycles. Liquidity is primarily provided by arbitrage desks, market makers, and high-turnover strategies that continuously quote around iNAV.

### US Equity Backing

Products backed by US equities hold stocks, ETFs, and assets held in custody accounts. Order routing runs through broker and API connections using RFQ, offchain execution, and onchain access layers. NAV-aware market makers can provide deeper quotes for these products because the underlying assets have well-established pricing conventions.

## Liquidity Routes by Order Size

Choose the route that best matches your order size. Larger orders benefit from routes that are closer to NAV and reduce market impact.

| Order Size | Recommended Route | User Protection |
|---|---|---|
| Small orders | DEX / instant swap | Fast execution with visible slippage and premium/discount to NAV |
| Medium orders | RFQ / MM quote | Multiple quotes may reduce slippage |
| Large orders | Primary create/redeem | NAV or execution-adjusted NAV may reduce AMM market impact |

## NAV State and Liquidity

Alloco uses a NAV state machine to reflect real-time data quality, market conditions, and operational status. The current NAV state directly determines which trading routes are available for a product. You can see the current state on the product page.

| State | Meaning | Allowed Actions | Restrictions |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fresh | Data current, prices normal, holdings reconciled | Normal trading, RFQ, create/redeem | Regular limits and fees |
| Stale | NAV or key price update delayed | Small trades and limited RFQ | Restrict large create/redeem, widen spread |
| Frozen | Underlying market closed or halted | Small trades or view-only | Suspend large instant create/redeem |
| Degraded | Data-source conflict or abnormal price | Limited trading and limited redemption | Increase haircut, suspend high-risk paths |
| Emergency | Oracle failure, custody event, extreme deviation | Usually view-only or redeem-only | Suspend secondary trading, RFQ and creation |

<Note>
  Liquidity may disappear during stress events. In Emergency state, creation and secondary trading may be suspended to protect the asset pool and existing holders.
</Note>

## Premium and Discount

Every Alloco product page displays a **premium/discount indicator** that shows how the current market price compares to iNAV.

- A **premium** means the market price is above iNAV — you are paying more than the calculated value of the underlying assets.
- A **discount** means the market price is below iNAV — you may be acquiring units for less than their calculated value.

Large premiums or discounts create arbitrage opportunities for authorized participants (APs). When APs act on these opportunities by creating or redeeming units at or near NAV, the resulting supply adjustment pushes the market price back toward iNAV over time. This mechanism helps keep secondary market prices aligned with the underlying value of the product.
