The strategy focuses on capturing market inefficiencies across crypto markets through automated execution, statistical models and disciplined risk management.

Rather than relying on directional market movements, USDA primarily seeks to generate returns from structural opportunities including:

- Funding rate capture
- Cross-market price inefficiencies
- Relative-value opportunities
- Market-neutral arbitrage strategies
- Liquidity provision opportunities

The strategy is designed to maintain limited directional exposure while seeking consistent yield across different market environments. USDA applies quantitative strategies across major digital asset markets.

The strategy combines:

### Funding Rate Strategies

The strategy captures funding rate premiums by maintaining hedged positions across spot and perpetual markets.

A typical structure:

$$
Return =
Funding\ Income
-
Trading\ Cost
-
Hedging\ Cost
$$

The strategy seeks to earn funding payments while reducing exposure to underlying asset price movements.

### Cross-Exchange Arbitrage

The strategy identifies temporary pricing differences across venues and executes trades when opportunities arise.

Potential sources of return include:

- Price differences between exchanges
- Market fragmentation
- Execution inefficiencies
- Short-term liquidity imbalance

### Market Making & Liquidity Strategies

The strategy may provide liquidity across selected markets by placing systematic bids and offers.

Returns may be generated from:

- Bid-ask spreads
- Liquidity incentives
- Market microstructure opportunities
